## Key Information & Responses

### "Epoxy Painting" vs Professional Epoxy

When people refer to "epoxy painting", they usually mean a solid colour epoxy coating. That's one style of finish.

A professional epoxy floor, however, is a **multi-layer system**, not just paint. A proper installation includes:

1. **Mechanical preparation** – Diamond grinding the slab
2. **Primer/base coat** – Penetrates and bonds to the concrete
3. **Decorative layer** – Solid colour, flake, etc.
4. **Protective topcoat** – Seals the system, UV-stable where required, optional non-slip

The decorative layer determines the final appearance, performance, and durability.

### Finish Options

#### 1. Solid Colour Epoxy (Most Cost-Effective)

**Our most popular Garage finish**

- Diamond grind + primer + 2 coats epoxy
- From **$50 + GST per m²**
- Clean, seamless look (can show slab marks more than flakes)
- Designed for vehicle traffic
- Durable and long-lasting
- Practical and easy to maintain

#### 2. Flake System (From $60 + GST per m²)

Provides a seamless polished-concrete / sandstone-style finish.

- Modern architectural appearance
- Highly durable
- Easy to clean and maintain
- More depth and character than a plain solid colour
- Best for hiding imperfections and gives an "aggregate-style" finish

**Note:** While a solid colour epoxy is possible, we generally don't recommend it in living areas. It can appear flat and tends to show wear more readily. Flakes offers a more refined look and hides marks far better over time.

#### 3. Outdoor Epoxy Systems

Epoxy can absolutely be installed outdoors. For outdoor areas we use a **UV-stable topcoat** and adjust the texture for appropriate slip resistance.

Flake systems are ideal externally as they provide grip while remaining low maintenance.

**All up: Epoxy + flakes + UV protection coat – from $70 + GST per m²**

Final pricing depends on:
- Condition of existing paint (whether full removal is required)
- Crack/joint repairs
- Traffic requirements

### Commercial/Industrial Options

For warehouse environments, we typically consider:

#### 1. Industrial Solid Colour Epoxy
- Most cost-effective option
- Can include non-slip aggregate
- Suitable for forklift or light machinery traffic

#### 2. Flake System
- More decorative
- Slightly higher cost than industrial solid colour
- Easier to maintain visually

**Note:** Large-scale industrial systems are typically more cost-effective per m² than residential installations due to size.
