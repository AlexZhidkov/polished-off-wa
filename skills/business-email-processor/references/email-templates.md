## Template 2: Exposure Explanation

**Use when:** Client asks about stone/aggregate visibility  
**Tags:** `#education` `#exposure` `#mpc`

---

One of the key decisions is whether you'd like to see the stone (aggregate) in the floor.

• **Nil exposure (burnished look)** – more industrial appearance with no visible stone.
• **Full exposure** – aggregate is visible, creating a more decorative and vibrant finish.
• **Minimal/Random/Salt & pepper** – something in between the above

Please note the achievable exposure depends on how the concrete was originally poured and finished. In some cases, older slabs need to expose more aggregate than new builds to avoid hydration marks.

Let me know your preference and we can guide you further.

## Template 3: Mechanical Polish vs Grind & Seal

**Use when:** Comparing finish options  
**Tags:** `#comparison` `#mpc` `#grind-seal` `#education`

---

There are two main options:

**Mechanical Polished Concrete (MPC)**
• We grind and polish the slab itself.
• Inside floors
• Densifiers strengthen the surface.
• Finish becomes part of the concrete.
• Seamless, durable, low maintenance.
• Penetrating sealer applied
• Ready to walk on immediately after completion.

**Grind & Seal (G&S)**
• Surface is ground.
• Outside concrete
• A sealer is applied on top.
• Lower cost option.
• Typically used in commercial or budget-focused projects.
• Requires 2–3 days drying time.
• Sealer may wear over time; needs reapply every few years.

For residential projects, we generally recommend mechanical polish for longevity and durability.

## Template 7: Shower Area Policy

**Use when:** Client asks about wet areas  
**Tags:** `#wet-area` `#shower` `#policy` `#warning`

---

We generally do not recommend mechanical polished concrete in shower recesses.

**Reasons:**
• Oils and soap residues can affect densifiers.
• Surface integrity may reduce over time.
• Increased maintenance requirements.

An alternative finish such as high-end grind & seal may be more suitable for wet areas.
